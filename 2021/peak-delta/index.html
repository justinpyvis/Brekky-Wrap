Peak Delta?

The most recent wave of COVID-19 cases has already started to decline in some European nations, despite relaxed restrictions â€“ e.g. the UK's 'Freedom Day' on 19 July â€“ and the rise of the Delta strain. Most importantly, hospitalisations remain well below where they were during all prior waves.

Digging deeper: The most obvious explanation is that vaccines work at preventing severe illness. All of the countries charted above have a full vaccination rate over 50%, equating to >65% of adults. However, it's not the only explanation â€“ it's summer, so people are outdoors which naturally reduces transmission. School is out, so students are not being tested (only mandatory when schools are open). There could also be some who simply started voluntarily socially distancing once case numbers hit a certain threshold.

But none of those, other than effective vaccines, can explain the fact that only in Spain have hospitalisations reached even 20% of their peak despite soaring cases in many countries. Now that's not to say they won't â€“ the real test will be when the autumn cool kicks in, schools return and people increasingly interact indoors.

Why this matters: As has been the case throughout the pandemic, Australia has a several month head start in terms of adapting policy to the virus. While our vaccination rollout has been abysmal, when we eventually get through winter and approach The Freedom Zone of 70-80% of adults fully vaccinated, we should know exactly what to expect â€“ Europe will have already done it!
